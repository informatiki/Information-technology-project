Saint Peter in Italian Christian Culture

Saint Peter, one of the twelve apostles of Jesus Christ, holds a foundational role in Christian tradition. Regarded as the first Bishop of Rome, he is widely considered the first pope by the Roman Catholic Church. According to the New Testament, Jesus entrusted Peter with the leadership of the early Church, symbolically referring to him as the "rock" upon which the Church would be built.

In Italian Christian culture, Saint Peter is deeply revered not only for his apostolic authority but also for his martyrdom in Rome during the reign of Emperor Nero. He was crucified upside down on Vatican Hill and buried where St. Peter‚Äôs Basilica now stands.

Peter is often depicted with keys, a rooster, a fishing net, or being crucified upside down. Artists like Michelangelo and Caravaggio famously portrayed him.

SPARQL Query Progression

Initial queries retrieved both paintings and churches. The query was then narrowed to movable paintings explicitly named after Saint Peter.

SELECT DISTINCT ?artwork ?title
WHERE {
  ?artwork a arco:MovableCulturalProperty ;
           rdfs:label ?title .
  FILTER(REGEX(?title, "(?i)\\b(san|santo|saint|sanctus)\\s+pietro\\b"))
}
LIMIT 50

Later, filters were added to exclude prints and frescoes, and to include keywords like keys and rooster.

FILTER(REGEX(?title, "(?i)chiavi|rooster|gallo|croce|pesca|pescatore|barca"))
FILTER(!REGEX(LCASE(?matTechLabel), "muratura|intonaco|fresco"))

Metadata Gaps in ArCo

Several gaps were identified:

  • Missing arco:hasSubject ‚Äì even when Saint Peter is clearly depicted.
  • Missing a-cd:hasIconographicSubject ‚Äì symbols like keys or rooster are not linked.
  • Missing color metadata ‚Äì black-and-white reproductions are not distinguished from original polychrome works.
